# Pending: the third skill

`proofread` (Corrector) and `inside-frame` (Illuminator) shipped. The third skill is an
open decision, to be made by the tool-opportunity map (§F) of a deep-research pass on
the Collinses and the broader heterodox-thinker audience.

## Candidates

- **Copyist — extract & clean** *(front-runner; completes the scriptorium metaphor).*
  Take a messy source (scanned PDF, PDF→text, OCR, transcript) and produce clean,
  faithful, re-flowed text — stripping page numbers, running heads, shattered
  sentences. It's the upstream stage the other two want: clean text means fewer
  artifacts hiding real errors (Corrector) and reliable verbatim quotes (Illuminator).
  The proofreading strategy that seeded this plugin named "clean the raw extraction
  first" as its #1 extension. Testable for free: `praglife.txt` (messy) vs `PragRel.pdf`
  (source); also absorbs a recurring monthly planner-OCR pass.

- **Indexer — auto book_vocabulary + quote-bank.** Pull a book's verbatim key terms and
  a quote-bank to seed `inside-frame`'s load-bearing `book_vocabulary` block (currently
  hand-filled). Coupled to the Illuminator rather than upstream of all three.

- **Quizzer — auto-quiz on key terms & takeaways.** Intelligently chunk a text into
  sections, then generate quizzes focused on the most important takeaways and the most
  surprising statements. A learning/retention companion to `inside-frame` — turns a
  read into recall practice.

- **Fact-checker — adversarial claim-audit.** Extract a document's empirical claims and
  verify each (the Corrector's verifier, but for truth, not grammar). On-brand for a
  truth-caring audience, but harder to test and riskier as a gift.

Copyist ships unless the research surfaces a higher-value intersection.
